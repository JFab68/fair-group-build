About the Coalition

FAIR Group is a bipartisan coalition under Representative Powell's leadership, bringing together Arizona citizens, people who have experienced the justice system firsthand, families of incarcerated people, advocacy organizations, and lawmakers to build a shared 2027 legislative agenda.

Our Approach: The Eight P Framework

Effective criminal justice reform requires addressing the entire system, not isolated parts. The Eight P Framework provides the analytical structure our coalition uses to identify problems, evaluate solutions, and coordinate legislative action across every stage of the justice process.

Each "P" represents a distinct domain of the system. Sustainable reform requires alignment across all eight. Our six working groups map directly to these domains, ensuring no part of the system goes unexamined.

Police

Law enforcement practices and accountability

Pretrial

Detention decisions and bail reform

Prosecution

Charging decisions and prosecutorial oversight

Probation

Community supervision conditions and violations

Prison

Incarceration conditions and programming

Parole / Post-Release

Reentry support and supervision

Policy

Legislative frameworks and statutory reform

Power

Decision-making structures and accountability

Why 2027 Matters

The Arizona Legislature's 2027 session is our target. We are building the evidence base, coalition relationships, and legislative strategy now so that when the session opens, FAIR Group's agenda arrives with unified, bipartisan support ready to move. This is not reactive advocacy. It is deliberate infrastructure for lasting change.

Six Working Groups

Coalition members organize into six legislative working groups, each focused on a specific domain of the Arizona criminal justice system.

Criminal Code

Reviews Arizona's statutory criminal code for outdated definitions, disproportionate offense classifications, and provisions that produce unjust outcomes. Develops proposals for codification reforms that improve clarity, consistency, and fairness.

Prosecution

Examines charging practices, prosecutorial discretion, plea negotiation standards, and accountability mechanisms. Develops recommendations to ensure prosecutorial decisions are transparent, consistent, and proportionate.

Sentencing

Analyzes mandatory minimum sentences, truth-in-sentencing provisions, sentence enhancements, and disparities across Arizona's sentencing structures. Produces evidence-based recommendations for proportional, consistent sentencing.

Community Supervision

Focuses on probation and parole conditions, technical violation responses, and reentry support. Develops proposals that reduce unnecessary incarceration for supervision failures while supporting successful community reintegration.

Police and Enforcement

Reviews law enforcement practices, use-of-force standards, accountability frameworks, and community relations. Develops legislative proposals that strengthen public safety and build trust between law enforcement and the communities they serve.

Incarceration and Rehabilitation

Examines conditions inside Arizona correctional facilities, access to programming, educational opportunities, and the evidence base for rehabilitation. Develops proposals that improve outcomes for people who are incarcerated and reduce recidivism.
