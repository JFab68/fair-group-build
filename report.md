## HOW TO USE THIS DOCUMENT

This compendium is organized to serve six working groups—**Policing, Prosecution, Criminal Code, Sentencing, Corrections & Rehabilitation, and Community Supervision**—plus two cross-cutting sections: **General Criminal Law Education** and **Legislative Advocacy**. Each section contains curated, authoritative resources including statistical reports, white papers, advocacy guides, research papers, government data portals, and practical toolkits. Resources are tagged by type (Data/Statistics, Research/White Paper, Advocacy Guide, Government Source, Educational) to help users quickly identify what they need. All URLs were verified during research and are current as of the date of this document.

## GENERAL CRIMINAL LAW & LEGAL PROCESS RESOURCES

### Criminal Law Basics & The Criminal Legal Process

**Resource: Understanding the Basics of Criminal Law | University of Pittsburgh**
- **URL:** https://online.law.pitt.edu/blog/understanding-the-basics-of-criminal-law
- **Type:** Educational Guide
- **Description:** Comprehensive primer on criminal law fundamentals including criminal procedure stages (arrest, preliminary hearings, arraignment, trial), constitutional rights of defendants (Sixth Amendment right to counsel, fair trial, presumption of innocence), burden of proof (beyond a reasonable doubt), and common legal defenses (self-defense, insanity, duress). Essential baseline reading for coalition members who need a refresher on criminal law foundations.

**Resource: Arizona Guide to the Court System | AZCourthelp.org**
- **URL:** https://azcourthelp.org/topics/in-the-courthouse/courtroom-procedures/744-criminal-process
- **Type:** Government/Educational
- **Description:** Step-by-step walkthrough of how a criminal case moves through Arizona courts: arrest, initial appearance, preliminary hearing, arraignment, trial, jury instructions, deliberations, verdict, sentencing, and appeals. Arizona-specific and written for public education.

**Resource: How a Case Moves Through the Court System | Arizona Courts**
- **URL:** https://www.azcourts.gov/guidetoazcourts/how-a-case-moves-through-the-court-system
- **Type:** Government Resource
- **Description:** Official Arizona judiciary guide explaining criminal and civil case flows. Details the roles of prosecutors, defense attorneys, judges, and juries. Explains sentencing ranges established by the Legislature and options including probation, fines, imprisonment, and the death penalty.

**Resource: Your Guide to the Arizona Court System | Janet Altschuler Defense**
- **URL:** https://criminaldefensetucson.com/arizona-court-system/
- **Type:** Educational
- **Description:** Explains Arizona's court hierarchy from municipal courts (minor crimes, petty offenses, misdemeanors) up through justice of the peace courts, superior court (felony/misdemeanor jurisdiction), and the Court of Appeals. Useful for understanding jurisdictional boundaries and where different cases originate.

**Resource: Criminal Deskbook (Fourth Edition) | Texas Municipal Courts**
- **URL:** https://docs.gato.txst.edu/690802/Criminal%20Deskbook%20Fourth%20Edition.pdf
- **Type:** Professional Reference
- **Description:** Comprehensive desk reference covering criminal procedure, alternative satisfaction of fines and costs, payment plans, entry of pleas, and procedural safeguards for defendants unable to pay. While Texas-specific, many principles apply broadly and the sections on court procedures are transferable to Arizona advocacy around fines and fees reform.

**Resource: A Beginner's Guide to California Criminal Law Procedure and Practice | CEB**
- **URL:** https://ceb.com/blog/california-criminal-law-procedure-and-practice-criminal-defense-legal-procedure-guide/
- **Type:** Educational/Professional

Useful for understanding defense-side perspective and procedural protections that reformers should know.

## SENTENCING WORKING GROUP RESOURCES

### Arizona Sentencing Reform Research

**Resource: Reforming Sentencing Policies and Practices in Arizona | Cassia Spohn, ASU**
- **URL:** https://academyforjustice.asu.edu/resource/reforming-sentencing-policies-and-practices-in-arizona/
- **Type:** Academic Research / Policy Brief
- **Description:** Landmark 2022 ASU article calling for sentencing reforms in Arizona, which has the fifth highest incarceration rate in the US. Identifies key drivers: Proposition 301 (prison for first/second drug possession); "repetitive offender" enhancements; habitual offender provisions (life without parole for serious offenses with two+ prior serious convictions); truth-in-sentencing (85% of sentence before release eligibility). Recommendations: reduce mandatory enhancements, repeal Proposition 301 for possession, reduce truth-in-sentencing from 85% to 60% for nonviolent/drug offenses, revise habitual offender law, reinvest savings in community programs.

**Resource: FAMM Arizona Fact Sheet**
- **URL:** https://famm.org/wp-content/uploads/2020/01/FactSheet-memos_Arizona.pdf
- **Type:** Advocacy Fact Sheet
- **Description:** Key Arizona statistics: fourth highest incarceration rate in the country; $1.1 billion annual corrections spending; 42,000+ incarcerated; 26% nonviolent; 21% drug offenders; one of only states requiring almost all prisoners to serve 85% of sentences regardless of crime or rehabilitation. Documents FAMM's advocacy for SB 1334 (repetitive offender reform, vetoed by Gov. Ducey).

**Resource: FAMM Bill Summary: Just Sentencing Act HB 2270**
- **URL:** https://famm.org/wp-content/uploads/HB2270-Bill-Summary.pdf
- **Type:** Legislative Analysis
- **Description:** Summary of Arizona's Just Sentencing Act (HB 2270, Blackman). Addresses Arizona's mandatory minimums and repetitive offender sentencing enhancements. Useful model for understanding sentencing reform legislation structure.

**Resource: Just Sentencing Act - HB 2720 (2025) | Arnold Ventures / FAMM**
- **URL:** https://www.arnoldventures.org/stories/lawmakers-preserve-public-safety-while-reducing-long-prison-sentences
- **Type:** Legislative Success Story
- **Description:** Arizona's HB 2720 (2025) ended the crack-cocaine sentencing disparity. Passed unanimously and signed by Gov. Hobbs. Sponsored by Rep. Leo Biasiucci (R). Arizona became the 46th state to address crack/powder disparities. Demonstrates bipartisan sentencing reform success in Arizona.

### National Sentencing Reform Data & Analysis

**Resource: The Sentencing Project - Re-Punished for the Past**

Findings: criminal records account for major portions of lengthy sentences (63% in Maryland, 30% Minnesota, 45% Pennsylvania, 41% Washington). Identifies only six states with generous mandatory release dates based on good-time credits. Useful for comparing Arizona to national leaders.

**Resource: National Corrections Reporting Program (NCRP) | BJS**

- **Type:** Government Data

### Arizona Corrections Economic Impact & Reform Analysis

**Resource: Arizona Criminal Justice Reform: Savings & Economic Impact | Rounds Consulting / FWD.us**
- **URL:** https://roundsconsulting.com/wp-content/uploads/2023/05/AZ-Criminal-Justice-Reform-1.pdf
- **Type:** Economic Analysis
- **Description:** Comprehensive 2023 fiscal impact analysis of Arizona sentencing/earned release credit reform. Three scenarios modeled over 10 years. Most likely scenario (Scenario A): $1.4 billion in prison savings + $107 million in additional tax revenues = $1.5 billion total fiscal benefit over first decade. Annual savings reach $200M by year 10. Marginal costs per person: initial 10-15% ($2,780-$4,170), growing to 85-90% ($23,630-$25,020) after 10 years of population reduction.

**Resource: The High Price of Prison Growth | FWD.us**
- **URL:** https://www.fwd.us/wp-content/uploads/2018/09/The-High-Price-of-Prison-Growth.pdf
- **Type:** Research Report
- **Description:** 2018 analysis of Arizona's imprisonment crisis.
